Understanding HPV

HPV causes six types of cancer

Human papillomavirus is the most common sexually transmitted infection. Nearly all sexually active people will contract HPV at some point—but in some cases, it can lead to cancer.

80% of sexually active adults will contract HPV in their lifetime—most will never know it. In 90% of cases, the infection clears on its own within two years. But for the remaining 10%, persistent infection can lead to cancer, sometimes decades later. The good news: the HPV vaccine prevents 90% of these cancers and is now approved for adults up to age 45.

Source: CDC, American Cancer Society, 2024
